I am Yilin Fang, a first-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) at The Ohio State University (OSU). I am a member of W3CIL Lab, under the supervision of Prof. Carter Yagemann. I do research on the intersection of computer security and machine learning. Specifically, I am using machine learning for automated vulnerability discovery, analysis, and prevention.

Before joining OSU, I obtained my Master’s degree in Cyberspace Security from the School of Cyber Science and Engineering at Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST) in 2023, advised by Prof. Bin Yuan. It was my great fortune to work with Dr. Yueming Wu on source-code-level vulnerability discovery and clone detection. I also obtained my Bachelor’s in Information Security from HUST in 2020.
